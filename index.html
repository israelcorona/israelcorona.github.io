以色列中国学生社区如何降低疫情对学业/研究的影响

致亲爱的中国同学和老师们，
以色列政府日前发布了针对冠状病毒爆发疫情的对策，其中包括多项旅行限制，可能会对每一名在以中国同学和老师的学习和科研计划造成显著影响：

  • 2020年2月2日早些时候：以色列卫生部建议以色列人口和移民局针对来以前14天内访问过中国的外国人关闭海上和陆上边境。
  • 2020年2月2日晚些时候：以色列卫生部长雅各·利茨曼发布指令，要求人口和移民局拒绝来以前14天内访问过中国的、不是以色列永久居民/公民的外国人入境。

旅行指导

如何从中国以外的地方返回以色列

虽然官方的旅行禁令并不包括拒绝未访问过中国的任何国家公民来以色列，在实际执行上仍然出现过各种问题：

  • 有学生在约旦旅游被拒绝陆路入境
  • 有学生家属在法国的转机机场险些被法航拒绝登机
  • 有学生在美国被俄罗斯航空拒绝登机

需要注意的是，以色列政府和以色列社会的规章制度受普通法、犹太传统和奥斯曼法律影响颇深，因而发布的规定常常措辞模糊并且实际操作者常常有充分的裁量权。因而，即使以色列政府发布了某项规定，并且以色列方可以有效按照规定执行，但这并不意味着其它外国航空公司可以按照以色列的规则执行这些规定。因此，不建议按照以色列政府公开发布的一纸通知制定高风险的行程。

此外，在以色列的防疫工作刚刚开始。每隔几天，政府发布的政策将会有显著不同，因而尽量不要凭当前状况安排以后的事情。

旅行紧急状况的处理

旅行受阻等紧急情况的大致处理程序如下：

  • 和航空公司协商询问解决方案：退票、改签、重新预定。并且评估当前情形和最坏状况。协商过程建议录音并记下相关工作人员的姓名、工号等信息，但是录音的有效性以当地法律为准。
  • 拨打领事保护电话，并详细提供护照号码、行程、目前位置（机场、航站楼、柜台、航司工作人员）和联系方式等信息
  • 如果长居海外，立即联系海外工作单位和上级，协商解决方案
  • 导致旅行受阻的原因可能很复杂（比如登机时入境政策和落地时入境政策不同、政府和航司沟通不及时），大概率当时无法解决。因此应尽早做决定（原路返回、在当地暂时逗留等待）避免无谓的时间浪费和经济损失，同时注意保存证据以备事后追责。

一定要保持清醒的思路、畅通的通信渠道和昂扬而理性的斗志。旅行受阻时，对情况认识最清楚的永远是自己。只有保持冷静和清醒，才能够最大限度的配合各种社会资源解决当前问题。

魏茨曼科学院

截至2020年2月5日的情况说明

受困于国内无法返回继续学业的同学

魏茨曼科学院官方目前已经联系中国籍同学，掌握了中国籍同学的近期旅行历史。目前，魏茨曼科学院的管理层已经注意到了10名学生/博后、3名学生家庭成员目前因旅行限制无法离开中国。此外有十余名参加物理系寒假研究的学生、1名学生母亲和1名访问学生可能受外国旅行限制和航班取消的影响。魏茨曼学联和这些学生已经将自己的情况告知了校长Alon Chen教授和研究生院院长Gilad Perez教授，目前正在等候进一步通知。情况说明如下：

Dear Professor Alon Chen and Professor Gilad Perez,
Hello! My name is Zhuohui Zhang, a postdoc in the Department of Mathematics. I am sure that you have been informed of the coronavirus outbreak in China, as well as the travel restrictions issued by Israel, China, the United States, and many other countries. In the past 3 days, some of our students and postdocs, who are currently stuck in Mainland China or their hometown due to the travel ban, have formed a self-help group (with whom I have been meticulously corresponding with) sharing the latest information on the frequently changing travel restrictions, flight cancellations, and possible ways to minimize the economic losses and the damage to our scientific careers.
As of 4:00 am 2 February 2020, 10 of our graduate students/postdocs, with accompanying 3 family members(including a student in the Hebrew University of Jerusalem), are currently stuck in China. They probably will not be able to return to the Institute and continue their research until the travel ban is lifted. There is also one visiting student and a family member (who is 60 years old and her hometown was sealed off due to the virus) facing difficulties on visa extensions and cancellation of flights. Their names, travel plans and original itineraries are attached to this email.
We would also like to request the Institute to kindly help us on resolving the following concerns:

  1. The possibilities of returning/departing before the travel ban ends: A delay of a few months in research activities may have significant negative effects on the students’ and postdocs’ careers. Graduate students may also miss courses and exams due to the travel ban. It would be very helpful if the Institute can arrange for special permissions from the Israeli government to allow these students to return before the end of the travel ban, with special quarantine locations, medical aids, and grocery availabilities.
  2. Visa extension for students/family members who are unable to return to China: Due to the travel ban and aviation regulations, the flights between Israel and China are very limited in number and are also subject to cancellation. One of our family members is 60 years old, with no knowledge of English, and she will not be able to return to her hometown due to the travel ban. We would like to request the Institute to assist these students and family members, for humanitarian reasons, on obtaining visa extensions from the Misrad HaPnim.
  3. Special Academic Accommodations: Some graduate students may need special accommodations for their courses/homework/exams, not only for the Fall semester of 2019 but also for the beginning of the Spring semester of 2020, to minimize the negative effects on their academic performance. The accommodations may include remote classes, take-home exams, and other forms of remote teaching.

Moreover, we would like to request the Institute to take proactive measures to prevent discrimination and hostilities against Chinese students/postdocs, together with other students/postdocs with East Asian backgrounds. We may also need arrangements of psychological counseling for these students/postdocs who have undergone such massive chaos.
We have been corresponding with the Chinese embassy in Israel on our difficulties last week. However, there is currently no ambassador from China in Israel (the previous ambassador retired last December), and a new ambassador may not arrive until this turmoil ends. As a result, it may be difficult for the Chinese embassy to make an all-out effort to assist us with our difficulties.
As we have always been, we appreciate the friendly scientific environment and heartwarming assistance from our faculty members and staff. We are looking forward to returning to the Weizmann Institute soon and bring more contributions to the science community of the Weizmann Institute.
Best Regards,
PYM, LWH, ZLL, WTZ, WJ, LH, LCL, BQJ, ZSY, XJW, HQF, ZY

疫情对这些同学的学业影响还有待评估，并且有待魏茨曼科学院提供进一步的对策。

学校推荐的防疫措施

2020年1月31日，在以色列卫生部注意到疫情的严重程度之后，魏茨曼科学院发布了防疫的应对措施：

Dear All,
The Israeli Ministry of Health has decided to halt all travel to and from China.
If you just came back from China, and have no symptoms, a medical examination is unnecessary. You should stay indoors and avoid contact with other people as you would do with other flu viruses.
Please follow this advice even if you do not have symptoms of the virus. If fever and respiratory infection symptoms (fever and cough) appear within 14 days after the latest visit to China:

  • Seek emergency medical care in a nearby medical center or a healthcare clinic.
  • Before going to a healthcare facility, call and let them know of any symptoms and potential exposure as specified above.
  • Avoid public transportation.
  • Cover your mouth and nose with a surgical mask or a cloth.
  • Upon entering a medical facility, immediately tell the receptionist or the nurse about your symptoms and whether you visited China 14 days before symptoms appeared.

For further information please follow the Ministry of Health web page at the following link https://www.health.gov.il/English/Topics/Diseases/corona/Pages/default.aspx
We will send updates on any changes in guidelines of the Ministers off Health or the Institute.
Sincerely,
Weizmann Institute Management
